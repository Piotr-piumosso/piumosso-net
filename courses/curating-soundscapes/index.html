Who this is for

Three professionals. One problem they all share.

Every luxury event professional has watched a beautiful evening lose its tone because the music was wrong. This course is built around that moment — and what to do instead.

Wedding Planners

You create environments where couples remember everything.

You know music matters — but you've been making decisions based on client requests and personal taste, not professional knowledge. The result is inconsistency: some evenings work, some don't, and you can't always say why.

This course is for you if: you've ever booked a "classical duo" without knowing how to brief them.

Hotel F&B Managers

You set the tone for evenings your guests will pay to repeat.

You understand that a hotel's reputation is built in the minutes between service — the ambient quality of an evening. Live music is the most powerful tool you have for that. You've been underusing it.

This course is for you if: your live music programme isn't consistent enough to become a signature.

Event Directors

You're responsible for the total impression of an evening.

You brief caterers with precision and briefing decks. You brief musicians with a phone call and a Spotify link. This asymmetry is where luxury events lose their edge — in the gap between the standard you hold elsewhere and the standard you hold for music.

This course is for you if: you want to brief a classical ensemble the way you brief a Michelin-starred chef.

Most event professionals make music decisions on instinct. Instinct is expensive.

The wrong tempo during a dinner service drops the energy of a room in under four minutes. A repertoire choice that doesn't match the acoustic of the space makes a Stradivarius sound like a school concert. A musician who hasn't been briefed properly will improvise — and improvisation in a formal setting is visible.

None of this requires expertise to cause. It only requires the absence of knowledge. And that knowledge has never been packaged for event professionals — until now.

The Acoustics of Experience: How Sound Shapes Memory
The ten variables that determine whether live music works in a space — ceiling height, floor surface, room shape, ambient noise. Classical Music That Actually Works: A Practical Selection Guide
Repertoire selection by event type: cocktail hour, dinner service, ceremony, gala entrance, evening close. Common mistakes — and the repertoire choices that almost always work.

Timing: When to Play, When to Pause, When to Pivot
The event timeline from the musician's perspective. How to integrate live music into dinner service, speeches and ceremony moments without breaking momentum. Questions

Before you enrol.

Do I need any musical background to take this course?

No. The course is designed for event professionals, not musicians. You will not be asked to read music or understand theory. Every concept is presented in the language of event management — decisions, timings, briefs, and outcomes.

How long do I have access?

Permanent. All individual and team licences include all future updates. The repertoire guide is updated quarterly. The briefing template is updated whenever we identify improvements based on community feedback.

Can I expense this as professional development?

Yes. A formal invoice is available on request. The course qualifies as professional development in most jurisdictions. All purchases include a PDF receipt.

Is there a money-back guarantee?

Yes. If you complete at least 3 modules and find the content doesn't meet the standard described, contact us within 14 days of purchase for a full refund. We stand behind the work.

What is the monthly Q&A session?

A 45-minute live session with Piotr Górski, held monthly via video call. Participants submit questions in advance; sessions focus on the most-asked topics plus any specific event briefs participants want to discuss. Recordings available for those who cannot attend live.

Can I book Più Mosso to perform at my events after taking the course?

Yes. Course participants receive priority access to Più Mosso's calendar and a direct booking contact. Agency licence holders receive first-access notification when new dates open.
