The problem

You brief caterers with a briefing deck. You brief musicians with a phone call.

That asymmetry is where luxury events lose their edge — in the gap between the standard you hold elsewhere and the standard you hold for music.

What happens without this course

Wrong tempo during dinner service drops the energy of a room in under four minutes. Repertoire that ignores the acoustic makes a Stradivarius sound like a school recital. A musician who wasn't briefed properly improvises — and in a formal setting, that improvisation is visible.

None of this requires incompetence to cause. It only requires the absence of knowledge. And that knowledge has never been packaged specifically for event professionals.

What changes after

You walk into a site visit and run the acoustic checklist in eight minutes. You open a repertoire guide and choose music by event type, tempo and room dimension — not by gut feeling.

You send a structured seven-question brief to a musician before signing any contract. And when the evening ends, guests remember the atmosphere — because the music was chosen for this room, this moment, these people.
